What I do

A summary of my research work.

Star Formation Efficiency

As a graduate student at McGill Space Institute in Prof. Daryl Haggard's group, I am currently working on characterizing star formation efficiency (SFE) around the center of galaxies, with observations from SITELLE at CFHT and ALMA. Here is a spatially resolved map of the central region of NGC 3169, illustrating how these data could tell us about SFE. With more data samples, we wish to have a better understanding of why star formation is quenched in this type of environment.

Aircraft Ground Effect

From 2018 to 2020, I worked at the Aerodynamics Lab at McGill University, studying ground effect of aircrafts, supervised by Prof. Tim Lee. We did experiments to measure the behavior of wingtip vortices in ground effect with different boundary conditions and wing designs. I helped to build this set-up of moving belt with laser velocimetry that could measure and visualize wingtip vortices. (Thesis)

Publications:
-- Lu, A., Tremblay-Dionne, V., and Lee, T. (May 17, 2019). "Experimental Study of Aerodynamics and Wingtip Vortex of a Rectangular Wing in Flat Ground Effect." ASME. J. Fluids Eng. November 2019; 141(11): 111108. https://doi.org/10.1115/1.4043593
-- Lu, A., and Lee, T. (October 4, 2019). "Passive Wingtip Vortex Control by Using Tip-Mounted Half Delta Wings in Ground Effect." ASME. J. Fluids Eng. February 2020; 142(2): 021201. https://doi.org/10.1115/1.4044669
-- Lu, A., and Lee, T. (November 18, 2020). "Effect of Ground Boundary Condition on Near-Field Wingtip Vortex Flow and Lift-Induced Drag." ASME. J. Fluids Eng. March 2021; 143(3): 031301. https://doi.org/10.1115/1.4048875

MEMS

As an undergraduate student at McGill University, I worked with Prof. Xinyu Liu (at University of Toronto now) developing various types of micro-electromechanical systems (MEMS). I was involved in projects developing paper-based nanowire sensors and 3D printable electronically conductive materials.

Publications:
-- X. Li, Y. Wang, A. Lu and X. Liu, "Controllable Hydrothermal Growth of ZnO Nanowires on Cellulose Paper for Flexible Sensors and Electronics," in IEEE Sensors Journal, vol. 15, no. 11, pp. 6100-6107, Nov. 2015, doi: 10.1109/JSEN.2015.2450179.
